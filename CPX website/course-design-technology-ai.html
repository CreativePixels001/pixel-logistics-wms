FLAGSHIP COURSE

Understanding Design & Technology in the AI Era

A conceptual course designed for professionals, educators, and curious learners who want to understand modern design, systems, and AI — without learning tools or software.

Self-paced
Small Groups / 1:1
Discussion-Based

What You Will Understand

Core Concepts

  • How design thinking shapes modern technology and why it matters beyond aesthetics
  • Why interfaces feel simple but systems underneath are incredibly complex
  • How AI and prompts actually work — demystifying the "magic" of generative tools
  • The psychology behind user behavior and decision-making in digital spaces
  • How humans and AI collaborate meaningfully in creative and analytical work

Learning Format

  • Conversational sessions — Not lectures, but dialogues that encourage thinking
  • 1:1 or small groups — Intimate settings where your questions matter
  • Reflection-based learning — Time to process, not just consume
  • Real-world examples — Banking, healthcare, governance, everyday apps
  • No tools required — Pure conceptual understanding

Course Curriculum

01 Introduction: Design in the Age of AI

  • What is design beyond screens and colors?
  • Evolution from print to digital to AI-driven interfaces
  • Why understanding systems matters more than tools
  • The role of design in shaping human behavior

02 How Interfaces Work

  • The psychology of buttons, menus, and navigation
  • Mental models: How users think vs. how systems work
  • Case study: Why some apps feel "right" and others don't
  • Trust and clarity in digital experiences

03 Understanding AI & Prompts

  • How AI actually processes information (without technical jargon)
  • Why prompts matter — the art of asking the right questions
  • Limitations and possibilities of generative AI
  • Human + AI collaboration: Who does what?

04 Systems Thinking

  • How large systems (banking, healthcare) are designed
  • Balancing simplicity for users with complexity for systems
  • Security, privacy, and trust in modern platforms
  • Why good design is invisible

05 Design in the Real World

  • Case studies: Government portals, e-commerce, health apps
  • What makes digital experiences accessible to all
  • Designing for trust and inclusivity
  • The future: Where design and AI are heading

Course Information

  • Format Discussion-Based

  • Group Size 1:1 or Small
  • Prerequisites None
  • Tools Needed None

Who This Is For

  • ✓ Professionals curious about design and tech
  • ✓ Educators wanting to understand modern systems
  • ✓ Anyone who wants to engage confidently with AI
  • ✓ Those who value understanding over speed
